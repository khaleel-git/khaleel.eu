Part B: Short Answers

28. Before puberty, the apocrine sweat glands—which secrete a rich liquid containing proteins and fats—do not yet fully work. Does this mean apocrine sweat does not affect puberty humans? Briefly explain your answer.

Key Point: Apocrine glands are associated with emotional sweating and pheromones, which become active at puberty. Their inactivity before puberty means they do not contribute to body odor or specific emotional sweat responses in children.

29. Name at least three different measures that can be taken to deal with artefacts in EEG recordings, either before or after recording.

Examples: 1. Skin preparation (scrubbing) before recording. 2. Filtering (High/Low/Notch) after recording. 3. Artifact Rejection (removing bad epochs). 4. ICA (Independent Component Analysis).

30. Compare EEG to fNIRS. List three relevant attributes and briefly explain each method's properties with respect to these attributes.

Comparison Key:

EEG:

  • • Temporal Resolution: Excellent (ms)
  • • Spatial Resolution: Poor (blurring)
  • • Signal Source: Electrical (Neural)

fNIRS:

  • • Temporal Resolution: Poor (seconds - hemodynamic)
  • • Spatial Resolution: Better than EEG (localized)
  • • Signal Source: Metabolic (Blood Oxygen)
